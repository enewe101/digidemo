So what's this all about?

Briefly.
Luminocracy is designed to support productive political discussion about issues that matter to Canadians. It combines agenda-free fact-gathering with the ability to create open letters to call on political, economic, and social actors for change.

But how?
Conducting productive political discussion is difficult for a number of reasons. Using this platform, we're experimenting with mechanisms that encourage people with different values and political orientations to cooperate and share.

A central feature in achieving this, is the distinction between normative and constative statements. A normative statement is a statement about what we should do. A constative statement is a value-free statement of fact about the state of the world.

Every issue involves both normative and constative discussion. But we separate these discussions, and treat them differently. Regardless of their values, people should, eventually, be able to agree on the facts. On the other hand, no amount of discussion would convince a person to change their values, and so we shouldn't try.

We focus on constative discussion in the wiki area, and try to foster a cooperative discussion about the facts. In that discussion, it's helpful to have people of diverse inclinations, in order to represent an issue fully. Value judgments and appeals to specific actions are totally prohibited here, because that will just take us into territory where consensus is impossible.

To support the normative aspect of issues, we use an open letter system. In this area, we express our values and call for specific courses of action by writing an open letter to the major actors involved in the issue (often politicians or heads of corporations). If you find a letter that you agree with, and you think is well-written, you can sign it. If you don't agree, sign a different one, or write your own. Letters aren't really open for debate. You choose what you support based on your reading of the facts, and your own set of values.

We hope that this will create an environment that is conducive to the respectful, diligent, and productive exploration of the important challenges and opportunities that we face together.

So, what now?
This is the very first iteration of our idea. It's rough, and we need your help to get it right. If you want to pitch in, then you'll want to join the Students' Society for Digital Democracy. We're just getting organized, so for now, that means joining the luminocracy Facebook group . We are building, and using, the platform together.
